        Nederland heeft een lange en diepgewortelde maritieme traditie. Zeelieden brachten verhalen en namen mee van hun reizen, die soms werden vereeuwigd in lokale benamingen.

Heb je van de Zilveren Vloot wel gehoord?

Twee neven van vice-admiraal Corn. Symonsse Loncke zijn ook naar zee gegaan. De oudste,

Hendrik Cornelisz. Loncke, was op de Markt bij de Raadhuisstraat geboren in 1568. Hij

werd ook schipper en trad in Staatse dienst (1604). In datzelfde jaar huwde hij te Amsterdam

met Grietje Leenaerts van Antwerpen. Twintig jaar later ontving hij de benoeming tot kapitein

bij de Amsterdamse kamer der West-Indische Compagnie. De Heren XIX gaven hem in

datzelfde jaar het opperbevel over hun vloot, maar Loncke weigerde. Wel hielp hij in 1626

mee, om Algerijnse zeerovers te straffen, die het onze koopvaarders keer op keer lastig

maakten. Maar voor een tocht naar Brazilië was hij niet te vinden. Toen werd hij met een

andere opdracht belast.

Alle schatten aan zilver, goud, huiden, edelstenen en campêchehout, die Spanje in zijn

koloniën verzamelde, werd eenmaal per jaar naar Spanje overgebracht, want daarmee betaalde

Spanje de oorlog tegen de Republiek. Deze vloot was natuurlijk zwaar bewapend. Als de

vloot van onze Republiek zo'n zilvervloot wist te bemachtigen, dan kon van de opbrengst de

oorlog tegen hetzelfde Spanje worden bekostigd.

Hendrik Loncke kreeg de opdracht om onder het opperbevel van generaal Piet Heyn naar

Cuba in de Caribische Zee te varen. Deer moesten ze de Zilvervloot van dat jaar (1628)

veroveren.

Al spoedig voeren ze uit met een twintigtal schepen en elf jachten. Op die schepen waren

tezamen wel 700 kanonnen en 4000 matrozen. Piet Heyn was aan boord van het

admiraalschip "De Amsterdam", terwijl admiraal Loncke zich op het schip "De Hollandtsche

Tuyn" bevond. Dit laatste schip had een tonnage van 400 last of 800 ton. 130 Matrozen

zorgden voor de vaart, terwijl 67 soldaten de 12 metalen en 24 ijzeren vuurmonden hadden te

verzorgen.

Op 22 augustus arriveerde de vloot op de juiste plaats in de Caribische Zee, zodat de

Spanjaarden, als ze uit Vera Crux en Cartagena kwamen, onmogelijk naar het Noorden

konden ontsnappen.

Maar al wat er kwam, geen zilvervloot. Piet Heyn en Loncke besloten de winter in Mexico te

gaan doorbrengen. Maar... in de nacht van 7 op 8 September ontdekten de matrozen vanuit het

kraaiennest de lang verwachte vloot: 15 grote schepen. De Spaanse admiraal gaf enkele

schepen, die niet zo'n rijke lading hadden, aan de Hollanders prijs. Hij hoopte te ontkomen

naar de haven van Havana, terwijl de Hollanders bezig waren de prijsschepen in bezit te

nemen. Maar de zeestroming was de Spanjaarden niet goedgezind. Piet Heyn zond vice-

admiraal Banckert met 5 schepen achter de Spanjolen aan. Hijzelf en Loncke sneden de vlucht

naar Havana af. Nu bleef de Spaanse admiraal Benavides niets anders over dan de baai van

Matanzas binnen te vluchten. Daar liepen zijn grote schepen nog vast op twee zandbanken.

Piet Heyn begaf zich aan boord bij admiraal Loncke op "De Hollandtsche Tuyn". Allereerst

werd het Spaanse admiraalschip genomen. Een matroos klom langs een afhangend touw aan

boord. Er volg
de een kort gevecht.

De Spanjolen waren al bezig kostbare lading in kleine bootjes aan land te brengen. Benavides

zelf had zich van zijn admiraalsuniform ontdaan, en vluchtte aan land in burgerkleding.

Vijf dagen zijn er nodig geweest om alle schatten van de schepen te halen en te noteren. Er

was veel zilver bij, maar ook kostbare gouden sieraden, barnstenen rozenkransen, gouden

kruisen en ketens. Alles bij elkaar een waarde van 11.509.523 gulden. De mensen in het

moederland waren dol van vreugde. In het begin van 1629 kwam de vloot in de Nederlanden

aan. Beide admiraals werden feestelijk ontvangen. Zowel van de Staten Generaal als van

Frederik Hendrik ontvingen beiden een kostbare herinneringsmedaille aan een gouden keten

bevestigd, waarop de baai van Matanzas stond afgebeeld.

In hetzelfde jaar kreeg admiraal Hendrik Cornelisz. Loncke het opperbevel over de gehele

vloot der W.I.C. De eerste opdracht, welke hij daarna van de Heren XIX kreeg, was met een

aantal schepen naar Brazilië te varen,om daar Olinda en de strandwal (recief) van

Pernambuco te veroveren. In het begin van Juli vertrok admiraal Loncke met een groot

eskader uit de Hollandse en Zeeuwse havens.Drie maanden lang moest hij bij de Kaap

Verdische eilanden op versterking wachten, die maar uitbleef. Dit kwam door de toestand in

de Nederlanden. Terwijl Frederik Hendrik 's-Hertogenbosch omsingelde, viel het keizerlijke

leger Utrecht binnen. Daarom waren soldaten èn munitie eerst in het moederland nodig. Maar

Bonn werd veroverd, het leger verdreven en Den Bosch ingenomen. Een groot aantal schepen

voer uit ter versterking van de nog altijd wachtende vloot.

Het ging niet voorspoedig. Weldra kreeg de vloot met windstilte en ziekte te maken. In de

week tussen Kerstmis en Nieuwjaar (1929/1930) waren er 800 zieken en 2 doden. Op 13

Januari 1930 bedoeg het aantal zieken 1200 en dat der doden 246. Als op 3 Februari de

Braziliaanse kust in zicht komt, zijn er nog maar 2515 matrozen van de 3780 en 2325 soldaten

van de 3500. De rest was ziek of dood.

In de strijd om de verovering van de kust heeft zich ook verdienstelijk gemaakt een neef van

de admiraal, Cornelis Cornelisz. Loncke. Hij was in 1586 te Roosendaal geboren. Bij de

tocht naar Zuid-Amerika was hij commandeur van het Zeeuwse eskader. Eenmaal voor de

kust aangekomen werden vele landingen uitgevoerd. Olinda was al spoedig ingenomen. Met

de verovering van het recief van Pernambuco belastte de admiraal zijn neef Cornelis

Cornelisz. Loncke met de "Domburgh". Hij voerde hiermee een geslaagde landing uit. Na een

beschieting van twee Spaanse forten konden de gelande troepen ook Pernambuco veroveren.

Het tekent de gelovige mens, die admiraal Loncke was, dat hij op 3 maart een dankdag liet

houden, "weghens de overwininghe, die Godt verleendt hadde", Om de nederzetting te

besturen stelde Loncke een koloniale raad in. Zelf vertrok hij op 7 Mei weer naar het

vaderland terug.

Na deze succesvolle tocht schijnt admiraal Loncke van de zee afscheid te hebben genomen.

Hij is in Amsterdam op de hoek van de Gelderse Kade en de Ridderstraat gaan wonen en daar

ook gestorven.

Quiz Answer Key

The Loncke family likely originated from Brabant. The name Loncke appears simultaneously in North Brabant (Roosendaal) and in Zeeland, specifically on Walcheren, Schouwen, Tholen, and in Zeelandic Flanders.

The Loncke family held local significance in Roosendaal, with members frequently serving in the magistracy and appearing in local registers. They also made important contributions to the history of the Dutch Republic by producing courageous seafarers who played a role in the Eighty Years' War.

Members of the Loncke family often served in the Roosendaal magistracy, which consisted of a bailiff (schout) and seven aldermen (schepenen) appointed by the Lord of Breda. They appeared frequently as common citizens but more often as aldermen in local and parish records.

One branch of the Loncke family seemed to have a longing for the sea, leading them to become bold seafarers. These individuals performed fearless deeds during the Eighty Years' War against Spain.

Honoré Naber's book about Piet Heyn and the Silver Fleet is noted for frequently confusing Admiral Hendrik Cornelisz. Loncke with his nephew, Commander Cornelis Cornelisz. Loncke, sometimes even on the same page.

A primary industry in Roosendaal in earlier centuries was peat extraction from the moors south of the town. Roosendaal's closer proximity to the sea at that time, with the Vliet being navigable by seagoing ships, facilitated the transport of this fuel, even to other countries like England.

Around 1640, the port of Roosendaal mainly featured numerous "cromstevens," which were smaller vessels characterized by a large mast, a small mizzenmast on the stern, and spritsails. Notably absent were the larger "spiegelschepen" with two or three large masts and square sails, which were equipped with cannons.

In 1628, Admiral Hendrik Cornelisz. Loncke participated in Piet Heyn's successful capture of the Spanish Silver Fleet. Loncke commanded the "Hollandtsche Tuyn" and played a key role in cornering the Spanish ships in the Bay of Matanzas, contributing significantly to the Dutch seizing an immense treasure.

The primary objective of the West India Company's commission to Admiral Hendrik Cornelisz. Loncke in 1629 was to once again attempt to establish colonies in Brazil, which was under Spanish rule at the time, following a colonization plan devised by Usselincx.

As commander of the Zeeland squadron during the Brazil expedition, Cornelis Cornelisz. Loncke played a crucial role in the conquest of the Brazilian coast in 1630. His ship was involved in the initial landing, and he oversaw troops designated to attack Pernambuco, contributing to the Dutch gaining control of key areas.

Glossary of Key Terms

Schepenen: Aldermen or magistrates in a local council, responsible for the administration and justice within a town.

Schout: Bailiff, the chief administrative and judicial officer of a town or district, often representing a higher authority.

Heimwee naar de zee: Homesickness for the sea; a strong longing or inclination towards seafaring and maritime life.

Republiek der Zeven Verenigde Gewesten: The Republic of the Seven United Netherlands, the independent Dutch state that existed from 1581 to 1795.

Tachtigjarige Oorlog: The Eighty Years' War (1568-1648), the Dutch Revolt against Spanish rule, which ultimately led to Dutch independence.

Vlootvoogden: Naval commanders or admirals; leaders of naval fleets.

Westindische Compagnie (W.I.C.): The Dutch West India Company, a chartered company that operated in the Americas and parts of Africa, involved in trade, colonization, and privateering.

Zilvervloot: The Spanish Silver Fleet, convoys of ships that annually transported vast amounts of silver and other valuable goods from the Spanish colonies in the Americas to Spain.

Cromstevens: A type of smaller sailing ship commonly used in the 17th century, particularly for trade along the coasts and in harbors.

Spiegelschepen: Larger sailing ships, often with ornate sterns (spiegels), typically equipped with multiple masts and used for longer voyages and sometimes armed for defense.

January 10 (year unspecified, likely after the September naval victory): Piet Hein and Admiral Loncke arrive in Rotterdam to great acclaim.

1624 uur:

Summer: Ambrosio Spinola and his Spanish forces gather in a waiting area near Gilze before moving towards Breda. Farmers near Breda move their livestock and grain into the city for safety, but some return home due to Spinola's initial hesitation.

July 25: The Spanish army sets up near Gilze.

August 27: Spinola unexpectedly begins to encircle Breda in stages, allowing some outer residents to flee into the city with their belongings.

Spinola decides to besiege Breda not by force, but by encirclement and starvation, despite advice against it due to Breda's strong fortifications, geographical location, and garrison.

The Dutch States Army, under Maurice of Nassau, gathers near Made to deter a Spanish assault on Breda by forcing Spinola to keep a large force occupied.

The Spanish begin constructing circumvallation and contravallation lines around Breda, with trenches, ramparts, sconces, and redoubts. Soldiers are tasked with the digging work, receiving extra pay for it.

The garrison of Breda, under Governor Justinus of Nassau, initially makes several sallies against the Spanish works, but these become less frequent. Concerns arise about the garrison's reliability and the governor's confidence in them.

The city of Breda takes measures to conserve resources and even issues emergency coinage ("BREDA. OBSESSA. 1625.") made from citizens' silverware.

Spinola issues a warning that those fleeing Breda will be forced back or hanged, though desertions still occur.

The Dutch plan to relieve Breda by cutting off Spanish supply lines from Antwerp fails due to unsuccessful attempts to capture the city.

Spanish forces build fortified camps in Gilze, Chaam, and Baarle and construct defensive earthworks along roads to combat idleness among soldiers.

The Spanish begin building a pontoon bridge over the Mark near the Hartelbergen.

The Dutch States attempt to disrupt the bridge construction and the Spanish blockade of the Mark near Terheijden with captured ships and a fireship.

Late 1624 (November/December): The Dutch States, under Maurice of Nassau, plan to send flat-bottomed boats via the flooded Haagsche Beemden to relieve Breda during a spring tide and northwesterly storm ("Noroest"). Spinola receives intelligence of this plan.

December 10-12: The Dutch States prepare an assault on the Spanish positions near Terheijden using ships and soldiers from Zevenbergen, coordinated with a sortie from Breda. However, unfavorable wind and water conditions (lack of a strong spring tide) lead to the postponement of the plan.

The Dutch devise a new plan to cut through the low dikes separating the Mark and the Beemden near Terheijden to allow supply boats to reach Breda. This plan involves a coordinated sortie from Breda.

The Dutch then plan to dam the Mark near Zevenbergen (at the Lamgatsveer) to raise the water level in the Beemden for a longer period, making them navigable for supply boats.

Late December: The Dutch States begin attempts to dam the Mark near the Lamgatsveer, employing boats and laborers. Freezing conditions cause a three-day suspension of work.

1625:

January: Repeated attempts by the Dutch to complete the dam on the Mark fail as the water's force repeatedly breaches the works. This happens up to three or four times.

The Dutch launch a second wave of attacks on the Spanish lines near the Hartelbergen, using boats loaded with explosives ("foco artificiato") to breach the dike and attempting to capture nearby fieldworks.

Spinola becomes concerned about the Dutch plans involving water and consults with engineers and master carpenters. He orders increased vigilance and reconnaissance.

The garrison of Breda closes the Groote inundatiesluis on the Mark and Aa rivers and builds up the bank of the city moat to prevent the accumulated water from draining away, intending to flood the surrounding Spanish encampments, particularly near Ginneken where supply installations are located.

Spinola orders the digging of a 2200-foot (660-meter) canal near the Breda fortifications to redirect the floodwater towards a marshy area (Abroek), preventing it from inundating his main camp.

Count Johan van Nassau (likely Jan van Nassau the Younger, commanding German regiments in Spanish service) attacks a Dutch redoubt near the Mark.

Don Giovanni de' Medici is sent to the camp of Count van Isenburg (near Hage) to order the digging of more trenches to divert the Dutch-induced floodwater towards the lower Mark, hoping to disrupt the dam construction near Zevenbergen and alleviate flooding in the Spanish sectors.

The dike built by the Breda garrison to retain the floodwater eventually breaches. The Spanish repair the breach to prevent the Breda moat from emptying at low tide.

The Dutch States, facing the failure of their water-based relief plans, reportedly consider a desperate plan to send 2000 men each carrying a sack of flour through the Spanish lines, escorted by musketeers. This plan is not executed.

The Dutch States Army under Maurice of Nassau gathers near Geertruidenberg, causing uncertainty among the Spanish about his intentions. A standoff occurs in October 1624 where neither side initiates a decisive battle.

Spring 1625: Despite Dutch recruiting efforts, Spinola also receives reinforcements. A major Dutch relief attempt is deemed unlikely to succeed.

By May 1625: Food supplies within Breda are dwindling, expected to last only until the end of the month.

May 1625: During a Dutch attack on Terheijden, the low water level in the Zegge makes the area passable for infantry, despite Spanish defensive ditches.

June 5: Breda surrenders to the Spanish forces under Ambrosio Spinola after a prolonged siege. The surrender terms are relatively lenient, allowing the garrison to leave with their weapons and baggage.

vertalen nederlands Based on the sources provided, the Spaanse troepen (Spanish troops) played the central role in the Beleg van Breda (Siege of Breda) of 1624-1625. The sources offer several insights into their composition, organization, tasks, and challenges during this significant event.

Composition of the Spanish Army:

•

The

Spanish army during the siege was not solely composed of soldiers from

Spain. It included individuals from various regions, with an estimated

ratio of one "real" Spaniard to six foreign mercenaries

.

•

The term "Spanjaard" was used conveniently to refer to anyone serving in the royal Spanish or "koningsgezinde" (royalist) army

.

•

These foreign mercenaries included soldiers from Germany, England, Ireland, and the Southern Netherlands, as well as those from Spain, Italy, and France

.

Motivation of the Soldiers:

•

Soldiers

on the Spanish side (and the Dutch side) were often motivated less by

conviction for a particular cause and more by the promise of the highest pay (soldij) and the perceived greatest chance of loot (buit)

.

•

It

was not uncommon for soldiers who had previously served together as

comrades to find themselves fighting on opposing sides during the siege

.

Tasks and Deployment:

•

The primary tasks of the Spanish foot soldiers (voetvolk) during the siege involved groundwork for the fortifications and the guarding and defending of the circumvallation against attacks from a potential Dutch relief army or sallies from the Bredase garrison

.

•

In the supply corridor, the foot soldiers were responsible for guarding and defending strongholds and escorting convoys against Dutch raids

.

•

A significant portion of the foot soldiers was held in a central reserve to provide assistance either in the circumvallation or in the supply corridor as needed

.

•

The Spanish forces were organized geographically around Breda into different "vakken" (sectors), each with its own "legerkamp" (army camp), except for the Hartelbergen, which was considered a "steunpunt" (strongpoint)

.

These vakken included Ginneken (with Spinola's camp), Teteringen

(Balançon's camp), Hage (Isenburg's camp), and Terheijden (divided into

the Hartelbergen sector with Baglione's steunpunt and the Terheijden

sector with Roma's camp).

Strength of the Spanish Army:

•

Accurate calculation of the Spanish army's strength is difficult due to incomplete and sometimes contradictory data

.

•

However, estimations for May 2, 1625, suggest approximately 25,000 men in the supply corridor, 25,000 in the encirclement, and 30,000 in the general reserve, totaling around 80,000 men

. This was described as "soo groot een heir, als men by mans gedenckenis niet in Nederlant gesien hadt" (such a large army as had not been seen in the Netherlands in living memory).

Command Structure:

•

The overall command of the Spanish forces rested with the opperbevelhebber (commander-in-chief), Marquis Ambrogio Spinola

.

•

Due to the large number of troops spread over a wide area, Spinola appointed vakcommandanten (sector commanders) to command troops within specific sectors under his supervision

.

•

Vakcommandanten could further appoint sectorcommandanten if their sector was divided by terrain

.

•

Notable commanders mentioned include Hendrik, graaf van den Bergh and Jan, graaf van Nassau, who succeeded Spinola at different points

.

Flags:

•

The Spanish armies in the Netherlands fought under a flag with a red knotted cross (knotsenkruis)

on a white background, which was the dynastic color of the Bourbons,

onto which the Burgundians had placed the red knotted cross. The Spanish

king, as Duke of the Burgundian hereditary lands, was also the lord of

the Netherlands

.

•

A uniform Spanish flag did not yet exist in 1624/25

. This flag, sometimes referred to as "knotsenvlag," is depicted on maps marking several Spanish siege works.

Challenges Faced:

•

The Dutch winter

posed a significant challenge, as many of the Spanish soldiers were not

accustomed to the cold and were not adequately equipped for a winter

campaign

.

•

Supplying the large Spanish army

in the field was a major logistical problem. Food and fodder had to be

transported over long distances from the hinterland (present-day

Belgium) via difficult roads

.

•

Dutch raids on Spanish supply convoys by state troops stationed in nearby areas like Roosendaal and the Langstraat disrupted the flow of goods

.

•

Poor road conditions due to rain, snow, and frost further hampered the transport of supplies

.

•

Potential for theft within the supply lines may have also contributed to shortages

. Pieri suspected that much food disappeared during transport.

•

There was an instance of a contagious disease (possibly the plague) within one of the Spanish companies

.

Interactions with the Enemy:

•

Despite being enemies, there were instances of relatively amicable encounters

between Spanish and Dutch soldiers, particularly between those who had

previously served together. They would sometimes engage in

conversations, share tobacco, and exchange pleasantries

.

The

information in the sources highlights the complex logistical and human

aspects of maintaining a large Spanish army during a prolonged siege in

enemy territory. The Beleg van Breda provides a detailed case study of

the challenges and strategies involved in 17th-century warfare.

Vertaling:

Volgens de verstrekte bronnen speelden de Spaanse troepen de centrale rol in het Beleg van Breda van 1624-1625. De bronnen bieden verschillende inzichten in hun samenstelling, organisatie, taken en uitdagingen tijdens deze belangrijke gebeurtenis.

Samenstelling van het Spaanse Leger:

    Het Spaanse leger tijdens het beleg bestond niet alleen uit soldaten uit Spanje. Het omvatte individuen uit verschillende regio's, met een geschatte verhouding van één "echte" Spanjaard op zes buitenlandse huurlingen.

Motivatie van de Soldaten:

    Soldaten aan de Spaanse zijde (en de Nederlandse zijde) werden vaak minder gemotiveerd door overtuiging voor een bepaalde zaak en meer door de belofte van de hoogste soldij en de vermeende grootste kans op buit.
    Het was niet ongebruikelijk dat soldaten die eerder als kameraden hadden gediend, elkaar tijdens het beleg aan de tegenovergestelde zijden tegenkwamen.

Taken en Inzet:

    De belangrijkste taken van de Spaanse voet soldaten tijdens het beleg waren grondwerk voor de vestingwerken en het bewaken en verdedigen van de circumvallatie tegen aanvallen van een potentieel Nederlands ontzettingsleger of uitvallen van het Bredase garnizoen.
    In de bevoorradingscorridor waren de voet soldaten verantwoordelijk voor het bewaken en verdedigen van bolwerken en het escorteren van konvooien tegen Nederlandse invallen.

Answer Key:

In 1587, Philips II planned the Armada to conquer England, aiming to then use England as a base to suppress the Dutch Revolt. When the Armada was sighted in July 1588, Vice-Admiral Cornelis Symonsse Loncke was ordered by the Dutch States-General to take 27 ships to England to await the Spanish fleet, operating under the English Admiral Lord Seymoer.

Breda was defensible due to numerous natural terrain obstacles that channeled potential enemy attacks. For example, the surrounding forests like the Liesbosch made passage difficult for cavalry and horse-drawn cannons, while the marshy parts of the Mastbosch and Ulvenhoutsche Bosch hindered the movement of foot soldiers, especially during high water levels on the Mark River.

Spinola's advisors cautioned against besieging Breda due to its strong fortifications, the marshy surrounding land, and the reinforcement of its garrison. Despite this, Spinola decided to besiege and starve Breda into submission, believing that a direct assault would be too costly and relying on the inability of the Dutch to break his encirclement.

The contravallation line was constructed close to Breda's fortifications to defend against sorties from the besieged garrison. The circumvallation line was built further out to protect the besiegers from a relief army attacking from outside. Both lines included earthen sconces (schansen) and redoubts along roads and paths.

Supplying the Spanish troops around Breda was difficult because their supply lines stretched from the Spanish-controlled hinterland (present-day Belgium) through potentially hostile territory held by the Dutch. Furthermore, the land routes were unsafe and required large escorts, and the roads were prone to becoming impassable during wet weather.

The initial state plan involved a naval attack up the Mark River towards Terheijden, combined with a sortie from the Breda garrison, to disrupt Spanish fortifications and potentially supply the city. Capturing Antwerp was seen as crucial to cutting off Spinola's main supply routes from the south.

The Dutch attempted to use winter spring tides and westerly winds to flood the Beemden (low-lying meadows along the Mark) to create a navigable waterway for flat-bottomed ships to reach Breda. However, the water levels often proved insufficient or the period of high water too short to effectively transport significant supplies from Zevenbergen to Breda.

The defenders of Breda attempted to flood the low-lying areas south of the city by closing the inundation sluice on the Mark and Aa rivers, aiming to disrupt Spanish encampments and supply lines, particularly around Ginneken. In response, Spinola ordered the digging of a drainage canal to divert the excess water away from the Spanish positions.

Spinola issued an order that any besieged individuals attempting to flee Breda would be driven back or hanged, suggesting he did not encourage desertion. Despite this, some still deserted, possibly due to a belief in Spinola's clemency, a lack of awareness of the order within Breda, or desperation caused by the siege conditions.

The terms of the capitulation included allowing the governor, officers, and soldiers to leave Breda with their personal belongings and weapons. It also stipulated that religious freedom would be maintained for the Catholic inhabitants of Breda and that prisoners of war would be released without ransom (only paying for their food).

1. Who was Cornelis Symonsse Loncke, and what was his significance in the Dutch Revolt?

Cornelis Symonsse Loncke was a Vice-Admiral in the service of the Dutch Republic during the Eighty Years' War (Dutch Revolt against Spanish rule). Born in Roosendaal in 1545, he had extensive experience at sea before joining the States' navy in 1568 at the war's outbreak. Loncke played a crucial role in the conflict, notably in 1588 when he was tasked with 27 ships to await the Spanish Armada near England, under the overall command of Lord Seymoer. Although strong winds and currents prevented his fleet from staying in the designated position, they later joined the fleet of Vice-Admiral Joos de Moor. His presence and command were significant in the Dutch efforts to resist Spanish power at sea. He is later mentioned as arriving in Rotterdam with Piet Hein after a successful endeavor, greeted with celebratory cannon fire.

2. What was the strategic importance of Breda during the period discussed in the texts?

Breda held significant strategic importance due to its fortifications and geographical location. Moreover, the ability to inundate the low-lying lands south of Breda by closing the sluice at the Ginnekenschepoort further enhanced its defensive capabilities. Its location also made it a key point in the ongoing conflict between the Dutch Republic and Spain, leading to the prolonged and significant Siege of Breda (1624-1625) by Spanish forces under Spinola.

3. How did the Spanish forces under Spinola besiege Breda, and what were their primary tactics?

Spinola opted for a strategy of encirclement and starvation rather than a direct assault on Breda, recognizing the city's strong fortifications and the presence of a sizable garrison. To achieve this, the Spanish army first encircled Breda by establishing army camps around it. Then, they implemented an "insluiting" (enclosure) by occupying the land between these camps to prevent undetected entry or exit from the city. To defend against sorties from the Breda garrison, a contravallation line was constructed close to the city walls. Further out, a circumvallation line was erected to counter any relief attempts from the Dutch States Army. Both lines included earthworks (schansen) and redoubts along roads to control movement and defend against attacks. Spinola also motivated his troops to undertake the necessary earthworks themselves by offering extra pay, which also served to alleviate the financial hardship of the soldiers who were often paid irregularly.

4. What challenges did the Spanish army face in supplying their forces during the siege of Breda?

Supplying the Spanish forces besieging Breda presented a significant logistical challenge. These supply convoys were vulnerable to attacks from nearby Dutch States Army positions, necessitating heavily armed escorts ("dubbelde verstercking van krijgslieden"). Additionally, the condition of the sandy roads was a concern, as they could become nearly impassable during wet weather or with heavy traffic. This made the supply lines the "Achilles' heel" of the Spanish siege, a vulnerability that the Dutch hoped to exploit.

5. What were some of the attempts by the Dutch States Army to relieve or resupply Breda during the siege?

The Dutch States Army, under Prince Maurits, considered several plans to relieve or resupply Breda. Another strategy considered was to use flat-bottomed vessels via the inundated lands (Beemden) during high tide to reach Breda, but this was dependent on favorable wind and water conditions (spring tide), which did not materialize as expected in December 1624. However, this effort was repeatedly thwarted by the force of the water and storms, ultimately failing. There was even a desperate plan considered to send 2000 men each carrying a sack of flour to cross the Spanish lines, escorted by armed soldiers, but the feasibility of this was highly doubtful, and it was never executed.

The Blaeu text mentions that in de tijdt van drie weken naeuwelijcks eenige lijftocht in't leger quam (in the space of three weeks hardly any provisions came into the army), leading to groote schaersheyt van lijftocht (great scarcity of provisions)



By April, there was a gebrek aan levensmiddelen (lack of food) in the bevoorradingscorridor (supply corridor), and they "bijna geen brood meer had" (had almost no bread left)

. Soldiers even resorted to eating horse carcasses.

•
Despite these difficulties, ondanks honger bij de Spaanse belegeraars bleef de insluiting van Breda gehandhaafd (despite hunger among the Spanish besiegers, the encirclement of Breda remained in place)

Interestingly, after the capitulation, the Spanish army was so weakened (partly due to malnutrition) that it had to be disbanded

. Coloma even noted that the besieged looked better than the besiegers.

The

sources suggest that the Spanish supply convoys faced persistent

challenges throughout the siege, requiring significant resources and

strategic planning to maintain the flow of essential goods to the army

surrounding Breda. The vulnerability of these convoys was a key factor

that the Dutch forces attempted to exploit.

Dit is een zeer gedetailleerde en informatieve samenvatting van de bevoorrading van de Spaanse troepen rond Breda. Hier is een Nederlandse vertaling van de belangrijkste punten:

De Bevoorrading van de Spaanse Troepen Rond Breda

De bevoorrading van de Spaanse troepen rond Breda was sterk afhankelijk van konvooien die essentiële goederen naar de belegerende troepen vervoerden. De bronnen beschrijven verschillende aspecten van deze konvooien, waarbij het belang, de uitdagingen en de maatregelen voor een veilige doorgang worden benadrukt.

Belang en Doel:

    De konvooien waren cruciaal voor het onderhouden van het grote Spaanse leger dat Breda omsingelde. Ze vervoerden levensmiddelen, krijgsbehoeften (waaronder munitie) en zelfs huisraad voor de officieren en mogelijk enkele burgers die het leger vergezelden.
    Het onderhouden van een gestage bevoorradingslijn was van vitaal belang voor het moreel en de effectiviteit van de Spaanse troepen, vooral tijdens een langdurig beleg. Tekorten aan voedsel en andere benodigdheden konden leiden tot ontberingen en zelfs desertie.
    Maurits erkende het belang van de Spaanse bevoorradingslijnen en was van plan Breda te ontzetten door de Spaanse bevoorrading af te snijden.

Bevoorradingsroutes:

    Aanvankelijk kwamen de voorraden vanuit Lier, via Antwerpen naar Ginneken.
    Naarmate de Spaanse troepen groeiden, werd ook bevoorrading vanuit Herentals en 's-Hertogenbosch noodzakelijk. Magazijnen werden in Herentals gevestigd.
    Er werd ook een iets veiligere konvooiroute via Rijkevorsel gebruikt, mogelijk om staatse overvallen te vermijden. Turnhout diende als etappeplaats op de route vanuit Herentals.
    De route tussen Antwerpen en Ginneken werd na oktober blijkbaar niet meer gebruikt.

Uitdagingen voor de Konvooien:

    Staatse overvallen vormden een aanzienlijke bedreiging voor de konvooien. Ruiters uit Bergen op Zoom patrouilleerden waarschijnlijk om de Spaanse bevoorradingslijnen te verstoren. Er wordt melding gemaakt van een opmerkelijke aanval door staatse troepen uit de Langstraat op een konvooi dat werd geëscorteerd door pontonniers.
    Slecht berijdbare wegen, vooral door regen en modder, belemmerden de beweging van konvooien ernstig. Voerlieden en kooplieden konden hun bestemmingen niet bereiken, en sommigen kwamen zelfs om door de kou. Een konvooi uit Lier werd gedwongen terug te keren vanwege modderige wegen.
    Diefstallen onderweg droegen ook bij aan bevoorradingstekorten. Pieri vermoedde dat "veel raven te vinden waren in de schaduw van de konvooien", wat impliceert dat goederen werden gestolen door degenen die bij het transport betrokken waren.

Veiligheidsmaatregelen:

    Konvooien reisden in konvooiverband voor de veiligheid.
    De wegen werden nauwkeurig op berijdbaarheid en aanwezigheid van vijanden gecontroleerd voordat een konvooi vertrok.
    Konvooien werden geëscorteerd door ruiterij aan de voor-, flank- en achterzijde. Aanvankelijk werd een groot deel van de cavalerie toegewezen om de voorraden te konvooieren en geleiden onder bevel van Hendrik van den Bergh.
    Voetvolk vergezelde ook de konvooien voor de veiligheid. In sommige gevallen werden pontonniers gebruikt als escorte. Troepen van de insluiting werden ook ingezet om konvooien tussen Baarle en Ginneken te escorteren.
    Geschut vergezelde soms de voor- en achterzijde van het konvooi om signalering en verdediging te bieden. Voertuigen konden worden gevormd tot lagers (beschermde kampementen) bij een aanval.
    Om de steun van de lokale bevolking te winnen en vroege waarschuwingen te krijgen over bewegingen van staatse troepen, dwong Van den Bergh strikte discipline af onder zijn troepen om molestatie en beroving van burgers te voorkomen.

Impact van Bevoorradingsproblemen:

    De tekst van Blaeu vermeldt dat "in de tijdt van drie weken naeuwelijcks eenige lijftocht in't leger quam", wat leidde tot "groote schaersheyt van lijftocht".
    In april was er een gebrek aan levensmiddelen in de bevoorradingscorridor, en ze "bijna geen brood meer had". Soldaten aten zelfs paardenkadavers.
    Ondanks deze moeilijkheden bleef de insluiting van Breda gehandhaafd, ondanks de honger bij de Spaanse belegeraars.
    Interessant is dat het Spaanse leger na de capitulatie zo verzwakt was (mede door ondervoeding) dat het moest worden ontbonden. Coloma merkte zelfs op dat de belegerden er beter uitzagen dan de belegeraars.

De bronnen suggereren dat de Spaanse bevoorradingskonvooien tijdens het beleg voortdurend met uitdagingen werden geconfronteerd, waarvoor aanzienlijke middelen en strategische planning nodig waren om de stroom van essentiële goederen naar het leger rond Breda te handhaven. De kwetsbaarheid van deze konvooien was een sleutelfactor die de Nederlandse troepen probeerden uit te buiten.
